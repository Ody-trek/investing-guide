# Chapter 6: Adaptation Guide for Chinese Investors

> The Permanent Portfolio was born in the U.S., but its principles apply globally. This chapter provides localized solutions specifically for mainland Chinese investors.

---

## Unique Challenges for Chinese Investors

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| **Capital controls** | Annual foreign exchange quota of $50,000 USD |
| **Lack of true long-term bond ETFs** | A-share market bond maturities skew shorter |
| **RMB exchange rate risk** | Allocating to USD assets requires considering FX |
| **Policy uncertainty** | Regulatory changes affect asset prices |
| **Different tax system** | A-shares currently exempt from capital gains tax |

---

## Solution A: Pure A-Share Portfolio (Simplest)

### Who It's For
- Cannot or prefer not to open an overseas account
- Smaller investment amounts (< ¥500,000)
- Low risk tolerance; prefer to avoid currency risk

### Specific Allocation

```
Pure A-Share Permanent Portfolio
├── Stocks (25%): CSI 300 ETF (510300)
│   Alternative: CSI 500 ETF (510500) or All-A ETF
│
├── Long-term bonds (25%): Ultra-Long Bond ETF (511090)
│   Alternative: 30-year Treasury ETF (511150)
│   Note: Duration slightly shorter than U.S. TLT; slightly less effective
│
├── Gold (25%): Gold ETF (518880 or 159934)
│   Alternative: Shanghai Gold Exchange gold savings account
│
└── Cash (25%): Money market fund + Treasury repo
    Recommended: Yu'ebao / Tianhong Fund (000198)
    Supplement: GC001 Treasury repo (204001); higher rate
```

### Historical Backtest (China, 2010–2023)

```
Annualized return: ~6.2% (RMB nominal)
Annualized volatility: ~8.5%
Maximum drawdown: ~−12% (2015–2016)
Loss years: 3 out of 14

Vs. CSI 300 (same period):
Annualized return: ~4.8%
Annualized volatility: ~22%
Maximum drawdown: ~−47% (2015 bull market crash)
```

## Solution B: A-Share + QDII Hybrid (Recommended)

### Why Add Overseas Assets?

1. **Geographic diversification:** Chinese stocks, bonds, and gold have higher mutual correlations
2. **USD asset hedge:** USD assets preserve value if RMB depreciates
3. **More complete PP logic:** U.S. market products are more mature

### Specific Allocation

```
A-Share + QDII Hybrid Portfolio (100% total)

Stocks (25%):
├── 12.5% — CSI 300 ETF (510300)
└── 12.5% — NASDAQ 100 QDII (159632) or S&P 500 QDII (513500)

Long-term bonds (25%):
├── 15% — Ultra-Long Bond ETF (511090)
└── 10% — U.S. Treasury QDII (OTC fund, e.g., ICBC Credit Suisse US Treasury)

Gold (25%):
├── 15% — Gold ETF (518880)
└── 10% — London Gold QDII or COMEX Gold QDII

Cash (25%):
└── 25% — Money market fund + Treasury repo (all RMB)
```

### Important Notes on QDII Products

```
⚠️ QDII quota limits:
- Each fund company has limited QDII allocation quota
- Popular QDIIs (e.g., NASDAQ 100) may trade at a premium
- Premium > 2% significantly increases your entry cost

How to check the premium:
- Look for "premium/discount rate" in your trading app
- If premium > 1%, wait for quota to open or switch to an alternative
```

## Solution C: Domestic + Overseas Dual-Account (Optimal)

### Who It's For
- Investment amount > ¥500,000
- Can open HK or U.S. brokerage accounts
- Willing to manage exchange rate risk

### Account Structure

```
Total assets: ¥1,000,000

Domestic account (RMB, 60%):
├── Stocks (15%):         CSI 300 ETF         ¥150,000
├── Long-term bonds (20%): Ultra-Long Bond ETF ¥200,000
├── Gold (15%):           Gold ETF             ¥150,000
└── Cash (10%):           Money market fund    ¥100,000

Overseas account (USD/HKD, 40%):
├── Stocks (10%):         VOO / 3033.HK        ¥100,000
├── Long-term bonds (5%): TLT / 2823.HK        ¥50,000
├── Gold (10%):           IAU / 2840.HK        ¥100,000
└── Cash (15%):           BIL / HKD MMF        ¥150,000

Total: Each of the four asset classes ~25% (distributed across borders)
```

### Foreign Exchange Strategy

```
Annual forex plan:
Use $50,000 USD annual quota (individual) = ~¥350,000

Recommendations:
- Exchange at the start of the year (annual quota resets January 1)
- Spread exchanges across different months (reduce FX timing concentration risk)
- Use bank "FX Bao" or similar tools to lock in exchange rates

Household stacking: Spouse $50,000 + Self $50,000 = $100,000/year
```

## Exchange Rate Risk Management

### Impact of RMB Depreciation

```
If RMB depreciates 10% (e.g., 7.0 → 7.7), USD asset value in RMB:
Before:  $10,000 × 7.0 = ¥70,000
After:   $10,000 × 7.7 = ¥77,000 (+10%)

Holding USD assets is itself a hedge against RMB depreciation!
```

### Historical Exchange Rate Reference

| Year | USD/RMB | Change |
|------|---------|--------|
| 2014 | 6.1 | |
| 2019 | 7.1 | −14% |
| 2020 | 6.5 | +8% |
| 2022 | 7.1 | −8.5% |
| End of 2023 | 7.1 | Flat |

**Conclusion: Long-term, RMB faces mild depreciation pressure. Holding a portion of USD assets is reasonable.**

## Special Considerations: A-Share Market Characteristics

### How A-Share Traits Affect the Portfolio

| Characteristic | Impact | Adjustment Strategy |
|---------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Policy-driven market** | Higher stock volatility | Consider reducing stocks to 20% |
| **Higher bond/stock correlation** | Reduced diversification benefit | Add overseas assets |
| **Mature gold market** | Gold ETFs work well | No adjustment needed |
| **Developed money markets** | Cash component earns decent yield | Use Treasury repos to boost returns |

### Suggested Adjustments for A-Share Version

```
A-share adapted allocation:
Stocks:      20% (reduced, due to higher A-share volatility)
Long bonds:  25% (unchanged)
Gold:        25% (unchanged)
Cash:        30% (increased, to take advantage of higher money market rates)
```

## Special Reminder: Don't Forget Insurance

The Permanent Portfolio manages **financial assets**, but complete wealth protection also requires:

```
Wealth Protection Pyramid:
    /  Permanent Portfolio  \     ← Financial asset appreciation
   /   Life + Critical Illness \  ← Personal risk protection
  /   6-month emergency fund    \ ← Liquidity reserve
 /   Primary residence (if any)  \ ← Hard asset
/________________________________\
```

Recommendations:
- First, get adequate insurance coverage (life coverage = annual income × 10 years)
- Keep 6 months of living expenses as an emergency fund
- Then invest remaining capital into the Permanent Portfolio
