# Chapter 3: Building Your Portfolio

> Starting from scratch, step by step — building your Permanent Portfolio.

---

## Preparation Before Building

### Step 1: Determine Your Investment Amount

**Minimum recommended amounts:**

| Account Type | Suggested Minimum | Reason |
|-------------|------------------|--------|
| A-share account | ¥50,000 RMB | Each of 4 assets ~¥12,500; ETF minimum 100 shares |
| HK account | HK$50,000 | Some ETFs have larger lot sizes |
| U.S. account | $5,000 USD | Fractional shares are common; low barrier |

**Capital Principles:**
- Only use **idle funds** — not your living expense reserve (keep 6 months of expenses separate)
- Never use borrowed money or leverage
- This money should be untouchable for **at least 5 years**

## Step-by-Step Execution

### A-Share Portfolio (Example: ¥100,000)

#### Stocks: Buy CSI 300 ETF (510300)

```
Steps:
1. Open your brokerage app → search "510300"
2. Tap "Buy"
3. Choose "Limit Order" (recommended) or "Market Order"
4. Convert amount to shares:
   - If current price is ¥4.00/share: ¥25,000 ÷ 4.00 = 6,250 shares
   - ETF minimum unit is 100 shares: buy 6,200 shares (round down to nearest 100)
5. Confirm and submit

Note: Trading hours are weekdays 9:30–11:30 / 13:00–15:00
```

#### Long-Term Bonds: Buy Ultra-Long Bond ETF (511090)

```
Steps (same as above):
1. Search "511090"
2. Buy the number of shares corresponding to ¥25,000
3. Bond ETFs have slightly lower liquidity than stock ETFs — use limit orders

Bond ETF NAV is typically around ¥100; price fluctuates less than stock ETFs
```

#### Gold: Buy Gold ETF (518880)

```
Steps:
1. Search "518880" (or "黄金ETF")
2. Current price ~¥3.5–4.5/share (fluctuates with gold price)
3. ¥25,000 ÷ current price = required shares (round down to 100-share increment)
4. Place limit order
```

#### Cash: Buy Money Market Fund

```
Option 1: Alipay → Yu'ebao → transfer in ¥25,000
Option 2: Brokerage app → Wealth Management / Money Fund → select R1 product → subscribe
Option 3: Treasury Repo (GC001)
  - Search "204001" (Shanghai) or "131810" (Shenzhen)
  - Note: Repos mature T+1; funds return automatically upon maturity
  - Rates vary with market; typically 2–3%/year
```

### U.S. Portfolio (Example: $10,000)

#### Step 1: Fund Your Account

```
Funding method (using Futu as example):
1. Futu app → Funds → Deposit
2. Select "Bank Transfer" or "FPS/Alipay"
3. Follow instructions to complete transfer
4. Usually credited T+1 (USD)
```

#### Step 2: Buy Each of the Four ETFs

```
Buying VOO (stocks, $2,500):
1. Search "VOO"
2. Tap "Buy"
3. Order Type: "Limit Order"
4. Set price: current price ±0.1% (close to market)
5. Quantity: $2,500 ÷ current price (e.g., $480) ≈ 5.2 shares
   → If fractional shares supported: enter $2,500 amount directly
   → If not: buy 5 shares (~$2,400); leave remainder as cash
6. Confirm order

Repeat for TLT, IAU, and BIL
```

#### Step 3: Verify Holdings

```
After all purchases, check:
✓ All four asset classes are held
✓ Each is approximately 25% (allow ±2% tolerance)
✓ Record the initial build date and prices (reference for future rebalancing)
```

## Choosing the Right Time to Build

### Common Question: Is Now a Good Time?

**The Wall Street answer: For the Permanent Portfolio, almost any time is a good time.**

Reasons:
1. The Permanent Portfolio is designed to not depend on market timing
2. Attempting to "time the market" typically backfires
3. Historically, buying at market highs still results in gains if held for 5+ years

### Suggested Approach: Phased Building

If you're psychologically worried about "buying at the top," you can phase in:

```
Build over 3–6 months:
Month 1: Invest 25% of planned total
Month 2: Invest 25%
Month 3: Invest 25%
Month 4: Invest 25%

Advantage: Reduces single-point timing risk
Disadvantage: Historical data shows lump-sum investing outperforms ~2/3 of the time
```

**Personal recommendation: Lump-sum if amount < ¥500,000; phased over 3 months if > ¥500,000.**

## Common Portfolio-Building Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Correct Approach |
|---------|------------|-----------------|
| Waiting for a market dip before buying | You'll never find the "perfect bottom" | Build promptly once you've decided |
| Using short-term bonds instead of long-term | Loses deflation hedging effectiveness | Stick to 20+ year maturities |
| Buying gold mining stocks instead of gold | Different correlation; strategy breaks | Buy physical gold ETFs |
| Forgetting the cash component | No buffer during recessions | All four components are essential |
| Adjusting the allocation ratios at will | Undermines the strategy's logic | Strictly maintain 25/25/25/25 |
