# Chapter 7: Common Mistakes & FAQ

> Real questions from real investors. Answers from a Wall Street perspective.

---

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: "The Permanent Portfolio guarantees you won't lose money"

**Wrong.**

- Historically had 7 loss years (~13% of years)
- Lost ~9.3% in 2022
- Maximum single drawdown ~14%

**Correct understanding:** It is a strategy with **very low probability of loss and very small loss magnitude** — not a no-risk guarantee.

---

### Mistake 2: "I'll substitute 3-year bonds for 30-year bonds — same thing"

**Completely wrong. This is the most common and fatal error.**

```
Why it matters:
3-year bond interest rate sensitivity (duration) ≈ 3
30-year bond duration ≈ 20

When interest rates fall 1%:
3-year bond: rises ~3%
30-year bond: rises ~20%

During recession/deflation, central banks cut rates aggressively.
Long-term bonds must rise enough to offset falling stocks.
Short-term bonds simply cannot do this.
```

**You must use bonds with 20+ year maturity.**

---

### Mistake 3: "Stocks have the highest long-term return, so I'll increase stocks to 50%"

**This breaks the strategy's core logic.**

- More stocks = more gains during prosperity
- But during bear markets (2000–2002, 2008), losses increase dramatically
- The Permanent Portfolio's core is **balance** — any deviation changes the strategy's nature

If you can tolerate higher volatility in pursuit of higher returns, consider a different strategy entirely — don't modify the Permanent Portfolio.

---

### Mistake 4: "I'll use gold mining stocks (e.g., GDX) to substitute for gold"

**Wrong. Gold mining stocks ≠ gold.**

```
Gold mining stocks:
- Are fundamentally equities; influenced by the overall stock market
- Include management risk, operating cost risk, etc.
- Correlation with S&P 500 ≈ 0.5 (much higher than physical gold's 0.1)

During the 2008 financial crisis:
Physical gold (GLD):  +5% (full year)
GDX (gold miners):   −26%

In a crisis, gold mining stocks fell with equities — not rose.
This completely defeats the purpose of holding gold.
```

---

### Mistake 5: "Rebalancing quarterly is better"

**No — over-rebalancing hurts returns.**

```
Rebalancing cost analysis (~0.1% per event):
Quarterly: 4× per year × 0.1% = 0.4%/year in friction costs
Annual: 1× per year × 0.1% = 0.1%/year in friction costs

Historical research shows:
Annual vs. quarterly rebalancing: < 0.2% difference in annualized returns
But quarterly costs 4× more

Conclusion: Annual rebalancing is optimal (or use threshold-based triggering)
```

---

### Mistake 6: "When the market crashes, I should move everything to cash"

**That's market timing — not the Permanent Portfolio strategy.**

Once you start "fleeing when it feels wrong," you've completely abandoned the strategy's discipline. Historical data shows:
- Retail investors who try to time the market lose ~1.5–2% per year on average
- When it "feels like a crash is coming" is often exactly when you should NOT exit (March 2020 is the perfect example)

**Correct approach: Regardless of market conditions, maintain the allocation — and only rebalance annually.**

---

### Mistake 7: "China's ultra-long bond ETF works the same as U.S. TLT"

**Not identical, but directionally correct.**

```
Differences:
1. Duration: TLT duration ~17–18 years; China ultra-long bond ETF ~15 years
2. Liquidity: TLT daily volume ~$3B USD; China ultra-long bond ETF ~¥500M
3. Rate cycles: U.S. and China interest rate cycles are not synchronized

Impact: China's ultra-long bond ETF is slightly less effective as a deflation hedge
than TLT, but it still works. When U.S. bonds are inaccessible, it's the best substitute.
```

## FAQ

### Q1: I only have ¥20,000. Can I build a Permanent Portfolio?

**Yes — the A-share version is the way to go.**

```
¥20,000 allocation:
- Stocks:     ¥5,000 → CSI 300 ETF (~1,250 shares)
- Long bonds: ¥5,000 → Ultra-Long Bond ETF (~50 shares)
- Gold:       ¥5,000 → Gold ETF (~1,250 shares)
- Cash:       ¥5,000 → Money market fund

The barrier is very low. ETFs trade in minimum 100-share lots — all of the above are achievable.
```

---

### Q2: I'm already 60. Is the Permanent Portfolio still right for me?

**Absolutely — perhaps even more so than for younger investors.**

- In retirement, capital preservation matters more than growth
- The PP's low drawdown characteristics are ideal for the retirement phase
- No active management required; only one action needed per year
- Suggested adjustment: slightly increase cash/short bonds to 30–35%; reduce stocks to 15–20%

---

### Q3: Inflation is high right now. Shouldn't I raise gold to 50%?

**Don't do this.**

Reasons:
1. Inflation may already be priced into gold (markets anticipate future conditions)
2. If inflation is quickly controlled (as in 2022–2023), gold can actually fall
3. You cannot know how long inflation will last

**The whole point of the Permanent Portfolio is "no prediction required." Increasing gold means you're forecasting that inflation will persist — that's market timing.**

---

### Q4: Can Bitcoin replace gold?

**Not recommended as a replacement, but a small allocation (<5%) is acceptable.**

```
Bitcoin vs. Gold:
Pros:
+ Hard cap of 21 million coins; stronger deflationary properties
+ "Digital gold" narrative in the digital age
+ Historically performed well in extreme inflation

Cons:
- Extremely high volatility (100%+ annualized); disrupts portfolio stability
- High correlation with risk assets (crashed with stocks in 2022)
- Insufficient track record (<15 years); deflation hedging unproven at scale
- Regulatory uncertainty

Conclusion: Gold has 5,000 years of history. Bitcoin does not.
As a core holding in the Permanent Portfolio, Bitcoin should not exceed 5%.
```

---

### Q5: Should I buy domestic gold or COMEX gold (USD-denominated)?

**Essentially the same — choose what's easiest to operate.**

```
Domestic gold ETFs (518880, etc.):
- Denominated in RMB
- Track Shanghai Gold Exchange prices
- Highly correlated with international gold prices

International gold ETFs (IAU/GLD, etc.):
- Denominated in USD
- Track COMEX/London gold prices
- Additional benefit of USD appreciation if RMB depreciates

The underlying gold value is the same — the difference is only at the currency level.
For investors in mainland China, domestic gold ETFs are simpler and perfectly adequate.
```

### Q6: What's the difference between the Permanent Portfolio and the All Weather Portfolio?

**Similar goals, different construction logic.**

```
Permanent Portfolio (Harry Browne):
- Four assets, 25% each (equal weight)
- Logic: Each of the four economic states is equally likely

All Weather Portfolio (Ray Dalio / Bridgewater):
- Approximately: Stocks 30%, Long Bonds 40%, Medium Bonds 15%, Gold 7.5%, Commodities 7.5%
- Logic: Equal risk contribution (risk parity)
- Higher bond allocation because bonds have lower volatility than stocks

Historical comparison (U.S., 1990–2023):
Permanent Portfolio: annualized ~7.5%, volatility 7%
All Weather:         annualized ~8.0%, volatility 8%
Very similar; All Weather slightly better but also slightly more complex
```

### Q7: When stocks are surging and I watch others making huge gains, I feel bad. What should I do?

**This is the "price" of the Permanent Portfolio. You must accept it.**

Reframe your thinking:

```
Wrong mindset:
"Stocks are up 30% this year and I only made 8% — I lost out!"

Right mindset:
"Stocks are up 30% this year and I made 8%, while protecting my capital.
If stocks drop 30% next year, I might only lose 5%,
while those who chased the rally might lose 30% or panic sell entirely."
```

**Focus on absolute returns, not comparison with stock indices.** The benchmark for the Permanent Portfolio is not the S&P 500 — it's "stable positive returns in any economic environment."

### Q8: Is there an even lazier way to implement this?

**Yes! Portfolio products are emerging.**

```
U.S. market:
- PERM ETF: A single ETF you can buy that auto-rebalances as a Permanent Portfolio

China market (as of 2024):
No public fund exactly following the PP strategy exists yet,
but you can use an "automated DCA" approach instead:
- Set up automatic contributions to four separate funds (each 1/4 monthly)
- Manually check and rebalance once in January each year

This means only 1 action per year (rebalancing); completely passive the rest of the time.
```
