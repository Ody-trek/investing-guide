# Chapter 2: Asset Selection

> Choosing the right instruments is half the battle. This chapter provides three complete solutions — U.S., Hong Kong, and A-share markets — choose based on your account.

---

## Solution 1: U.S. Brokerage Account (Optimal)

The U.S. market offers the most complete and liquid instruments for implementing the Permanent Portfolio.

### Stocks (25%)

| ETF | Name | Expense Ratio | Notes |
|-----|------|--------------|-------|
| **VOO** | Vanguard S&P 500 ETF | 0.03% | Tracks S&P 500; top choice |
| **IVV** | iShares Core S&P 500 ETF | 0.03% | Nearly identical to VOO |
| **VTI** | Vanguard Total Stock Market | 0.03% | Broader total market coverage |
| **SPY** | SPDR S&P 500 ETF | 0.09% | Oldest; highest liquidity |

**Recommended: VOO or VTI**

---

### Long-Term Bonds (25%)

| ETF | Name | Expense Ratio | Duration | Notes |
|-----|------|--------------|---------|-------|
| **TLT** | iShares 20+ Year Treasury | 0.15% | ~17 years | Most commonly used; great liquidity |
| **EDV** | Vanguard Extended Duration | 0.06% | ~25 years | Longer duration; more volatile |
| **VGLT** | Vanguard Long-Term Treasury | 0.04% | ~15 years | Low cost option |
| **ZROZ** | PIMCO 25+ Year Zero Coupon | 0.15% | ~27 years | Highest duration; purest exposure |

**Recommended: TLT (best liquidity) or EDV (purer long-end exposure)**

> ⚠️ Warning: You must use **20-year or longer** Treasury ETFs. Medium-term 5–10 year bonds are far less effective for this strategy.

## Physical Gold vs. Gold ETF

| Method | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Gold ETF** | Easy to trade, low cost, no storage | Must trust custodian |
| **Physical gold (bars)** | No counterparty risk; real ownership | Storage costs; harder to liquidate |
| **Gold savings account** | Bank-backed, low minimum | Has credit risk |

**Recommendation: Gold ETFs are fine for most investors. For holdings >¥5M, consider partially holding physical gold.**

---

## Golden Rules for Selecting Instruments

1. **Prefer index ETFs; avoid actively managed funds**
2. **Check the expense ratio (TER/management fee) — lower is better**
3. **Check AUM (total assets) — larger means better liquidity**
4. **Check tracking error** — should be less than 0.5%/year
5. **Never use leveraged ETFs** (e.g., TQQQ, UPRO)
